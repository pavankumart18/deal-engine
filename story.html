Part 1. Generation

Architecting Chaos

To test an enterprise agent, pristine data is useless. We needed to simulate the absolute friction of a modern global commercial deal desk.

We programmatically generated 100 full diagnostic opportunities across 9 regions, natively generating instruments ranging from $75K to $450K. But more importantly, we injected over 20 critical edge cases.

We hid "Ghost Contracts" (expired but marked active), misaligned currencies (CAD deals attempting to pull EUR pricing), and deliberately aggressive sales discounting aimed at triggering CFO escalations.

Part 2. Governance Logic

The Mathematics of Escalation

The core agent operates purely on deterministic rules. As it ingests the chaotic datasets, it normalizes currencies and computes baseline economics instantly.

Instead of merely rejecting bad deals, it dynamically generates three strategic paths per opportunity: Win Now, Balanced, and Value-Based.

Watch how it evaluates HealthCenter 0 (OPP-2000). The sales rep selected Scenario A for its 29% discount to secure the bid. The agent detected a European margin floor violation (sub-33%), threw a RED severity flag, and autonomously pivoted to Scenario B.

Part 3. Explainability

Synthesizing The Narrative

An algorithm that enforces limits is a tool. An agent that explains its reasoning to an executive is a partner.

Once the mathematical recommendation is secured, the final engine layer translates the specific variables—margins, regional currencies, audit rule IDs—into a seamless executive narrative.

This allows the final JSON packet to be routed directly to a dashboard or a VP's inbox, proving not just what to do, but why the autonomous engine chose to do it.
